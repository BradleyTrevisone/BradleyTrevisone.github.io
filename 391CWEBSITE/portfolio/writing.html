My Shakespeare Courses Work Portfolio

I deeply enjoyed all of my Shakespeare courses because of how extensive and diverse our readings were. It allowed me to see what themes, feelings, and ideas I want to play with a little longer...

Lacuna is my creative writing piece from my Advanced Shakespeare course that focuses entirely on the play Othello to explore 1600's racecraft. In this project, I wrote about how identity is formed from what we learn and how we learn we're also wrong, explore how people choose what they want to hear when others talks, and even touch upon how our identity and bodies are not fully our own possessions.

This final essay for my normal Shakespeare course was a personal reflection of ourselves in one of the plays. King Lear was one of my favorite pieces of art I've ever gotten to experience from all of its tragedy of aging, being rejected by family, and completely losing your identiy. However, I thought this play was an extremely beautiful rebirth that highly questioned human's natural state of being and what we've surrendered in order to evolve. This play seemed so desperate to be more than tragedy, that I wrote about how tragedies, void of happiness, still make themselves meaningful.

This is another assignment within King Lear that explores language and inventions as faulty tools. Using a setting hundreds of years before Shakespeares time, he's able to demonstate how internal wounds have become assosiated with present day needs rather than our natural human ones. An example being Edmund's deep struggle with being an illegitiment son when before the idea of pure identity was created, this would never have been an issue. It then calls to question when did we learn this to begin with?
