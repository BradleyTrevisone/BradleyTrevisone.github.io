Unity OnDemand Video tutorial

As part of my education on Accessibility, I created a video tutorial for Unity, which is a platform used by universities for computing and storing data. My task was to create a walk through for creating jobs that processes input data.

My first step was navigating through the program intuitively and took notes on the different ways my task could be accomplished as well as aspects I didn't understand. While I still never understood the specific differences between the science computing programs, I researched and figured out a way to communicate the importance to somebody that would understand the different operations.

In the video creation process, I used my music production background to organize my tasks. It was extremely helpful for streamlining the audio recording process, script writing, and the video recording. By recording audio and video in a separate programs, I was able to do a quick first take of myself doing the action while commentating. Then I could go back to rerecord the audio while following a script undistracted, and finally rerecord the video while splicing the audio to match the proper times to achieve a quality that would not be achievable if it was all recorded simultaneously.

My special touch was creating high quality audio through my Digital Audio Workshop, Reaper, by EQing the audio, noise gating excessive background noise, and softening the "B,P,S" sounds that cut through the audio if they are not filtered out with specific audio effects.

After finishing this project, I felt like I had a better understanding on the communication and technical writing components of accessibility content, what I need to know and what I don't need to know to create valuable content, and value the user experience for both navigating the website the easiest way and effort into the content that aims to benefit them.
