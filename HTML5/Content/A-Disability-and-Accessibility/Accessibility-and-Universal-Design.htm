Accessibility and Universal Design

Accessibility is beneficial for everyone and surprisingly common items or features began as tools to overcome disabilities like curb cuts, bendy straws, and even the typewriter and keyboard.

Accessibility has become the central value of universal design. It intends to make products usable by all people, to the greatest extent possible, without further adaptation or special designs, and this requires having accessibility in mind during early design stages. Incorporating Accessibility into Digital Spaces

When designing digital content, it's important to keep universal design in mind from the earliest stages of development. There is a natural lack of knowledge on accessibility, that's because most people are not constantly thinking about what others may need and most features are hidden from users.

Assistive Technology

The table below lists common assistive technologies with their function that must be accounted for in the programs code as well as be compatible with software and hardware. The information is provided by W3C.

Assistive Technology Purpose / Functionality
Screen Magnifiers Adjust text or font size, spacing, color, and sync with speech to improve readability
Screen Readers Read text aloud using synthesized speech or convert it to braille
Text-to-Speech Software Converts written text into spoken words
Speech Recognition Software Allows control of a computer through spoken commands
Alternative Keyboards Simulate keyboard input via head pointers, sip/puff, switches, controllers, etc.
Alternative Pointing Devices Simulate mouse movement and clicking functions

Interface Design

Interface design focuses on creating layouts, navigation, and interactions that are usable by everyone. Thoughtful design choices improve the user experience as a whole or expose faults and oversights when not considered or implemented correctly.
